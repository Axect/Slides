\(f(R)\) Theory

In GR, The action is

S = \int d^4x \; \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{R}{16\pi G_N} + L_m \right]

In \(f(R)\) theory, The action is

S = \int d^4x \; \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{R+f(R)}{16\pi G_N} + L_m \right]

 f(R) is an arbitrary function of Ricci scalar

The field equation of GR in metric formalism

G_{\mu\nu} = \kappa T_{\mu\nu}
T_{\mu\nu} \equiv - \frac{2}{\sqrt{-g}} \frac{\delta S_M}{\delta g^{\mu\nu}}
G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} R g_{\mu\nu}
\kappa = 8\pi G_N

\(f(R)\) Theory

The field equation of GR in metric formalism

G_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}g_{\mu\nu} f(R) + f_R R_{\mu\nu} + g_{\mu\nu} \square f_R - \nabla_\mu \nabla_\nu f_R = \kappa T_{\mu\nu}
G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} R g_{\mu\nu}
f_R = \frac{\partial f(R)}{\partial R}

\(f(R)\) Theory

T_{\mu\nu} \equiv - \frac{2}{\sqrt{-g}} \frac{\delta S_M}{\delta g^{\mu\nu}}

Conditions for the Viability f(R) dark energy models

\begin{aligned} &(1)\; f_{R} > 0 \quad \text{for }R \geq R_0 \\ &(2)\; f_{RR}\left( = \frac{\partial f_R}{\partial R} \right) >0 \quad \text{for }R \geq R_0 \\ &(3)\; f(R) \rightarrow -2\Lambda \quad \text{for }R \gg R_0 \\ &(4)\; 0<\frac{Rf_{RR}}{f_R}<1 \quad \text{at } -\frac{Rf_R}{f} =-2 \end{aligned}

Chameleon Mechanism

Modified potential generated by a point mass \(M\)

\Phi_{\text{tot}} =\; - \frac{G_N M}{r} \left( 1 + \frac{\Delta G}{G_N} e^{-mr}\right)

self-screened: \(|\Phi| > |\Phi_c|(m \rightarrow \infty) \)

not self-screened:\(|\Phi| < |\Phi_c|(m \rightarrow 0) \)

Unscreened objects with seperation \(r< \lambda_c\) interact through standard gravity and new force \(\Rightarrow\) called as the fifth force

Fifth force phenomenology

Because of screening mechanism,

  • Gas & DM have an acceleration (\(\vec{a_5}\))
  • Stars (usually main sequence) have no acceleration
  • Gas - Star offset
  • Dark matter halo - Galaxy disk offset

Galaxy Warp by fifth force

  • DM halo - Disk offset generates U-shape Galaxy warp.
     
  • Dark matter acceleration is given as:
    \(\vec{a}_{\text{DM}} = \vec{a} + \dfrac{\Delta G}{G_N} \vec{a_5}\)
     
  • Galaxy acceleration is given as:
    \(\vec{a_*} = \vec{a} + G_N \dfrac{M(<r_*)}{r_*^2} \hat{r_*}\)

Modelling

We should make a statistical model to constrain parameter \(\dfrac{\Delta G}{G_N},~\lambda_C\) from observations .

First, \(\vec{a}_5\) is modelled as Yukawa force from sources within \(4\lambda_C\) :

\(\displaystyle \vec{a}_{5,i}(\Delta G/G_N, \lambda_C) = \sum_{r_{ij} < 4\lambda_C} - \frac{\Delta GM_j}{r_{ij}^2} e^{-r_{ij}/4\lambda_C} \left( 1 + \frac{r_{ij}}{\lambda_C}\right) \hat{r}_{ij}\)

Second, to solve this, assigning halos to the galaxies with AM (Abundance Matching)
and assume NFW halo profiles and power law to create a first component of \(\vec{a}_5\).

\(\rho(r) \simeq \rho_{rs} (r/r_s)^{-n}\)

Modelling

Using this profile, we can obtain \(w_1\):

\(\displaystyle w_1 = -\frac{n(3-n)}{(n+1)(n+2)} a_{5,z} \frac{\Delta G}{G_N^2} \frac{1}{4\pi \rho_{rs}} \frac{(L_x / r_s)^n}{L_x}\)

  • With DarkSky-400 simulation, find halo catalog for each NSA catalog.
    • Restoring lower mass substructure by MC and DarkSky-400.
    • Generate 200-mock catalogue.
    • Each MC realisations of our model produces a possible \(w_1\) for given \(\{\Delta G, \lambda_C\}\) so that the full set of realisations specifies the likelihood.

Modelling

Rewrite \(\mathcal{L}_{5,i}\) with Gaussian distribution (of width \(\sigma_i\) for galaxy \(i\))

\(\displaystyle \mathcal{L}_{5,i} (w_1 | \Delta G, \lambda_C) = (1-f) \frac{\exp \left\{-w_1^2 / 2\sigma_i^2 \right\} }{\sqrt{2\pi} \sigma_i} + f \sum_{j=0}{N_\text{bins}} P_j \frac{\exp \left\{-(w_1 - \Delta G w_j)^2 / 2\sigma_i^2 \right\}}{\sqrt{2\pi} \sigma_i}\)

Now, set likelihood as follows:
\(\mathcal{L}_{5,i} (w_1 | \Delta G,\lambda_C) = (1-f)\delta(w_1) + f \sum P_j \delta(w_1 - \Delta G w_j)\)

  • \(i\) for each galaxies, \(P_j = N_j / N_{MC}\) (fraction of bin \(j\))
  • \(w_j = w_1 + (j+1/2)\Delta w\) : Position of \(j\)-th bin center.
