FERMI GAS Equation OF State

From Fermi-Dirac statistics,

\begin{aligned} n &= 2 \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\, f_k \\ \varepsilon &= 2 \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\, f_k E_k \\ s &= -2 \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}[(1-f_k)\ln(1 - f_k) + f_k \ln f_k] \end{aligned}
f_k \equiv \frac{1}{e^{(E_k-\mu)/T} + 1}~~where~~E_k = \sqrt{k^2 + m^2}

FERMI GAS Equation OF State

Also pressure can be obtained by

P = -\varepsilon + \mu n + Ts
\displaystyle P = 2T \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\ln \left(1 + e^{-(E_k -\mu)/T}\right)

FERMI GAS Equation OF State

Use \( T \rightarrow 0 \) approximation,

\begin{aligned} &f_k = \theta(k_F - k) \\ &n = \frac{k_F^3}{3\pi^2} \\ &\varepsilon = \frac{1}{4\pi^2}\left[ \mu k_F \left(\mu^2 - \frac{1}{2}m^2 - \frac{1}{2} m^4 \ln \left( \frac{\mu + k_F}{m} \right)\right)\right] \\ &P = \frac{1}{12\pi^2}\left[ \mu k_F \left(\mu^2 - \frac{5}{2}m^2 + \frac{3}{2}m^4 \ln \left( \frac{\mu+k_F}{m}\right)\right)\right] \end{aligned}

FERMI GAS Equation OF State

In EOS, we should hold two conditions.

  1. Baryon number conservation
  2. Charge neutrality

We want to find momenta which minimize energy with above two conditions.

\( \Rightarrow \) Lagrange Multiplier

FERMI GAS Equation OF State

F(n_n, n_p, n_e) \equiv \varepsilon(n_n,n_p,n_e) + \alpha (n - n_n - n_p) + \beta(n_e - n_p)
\displaystyle \frac{\partial F}{\partial n_n} = 0,~\frac{\partial F}{\partial n_p} =0,~\frac{\partial F}{\partial n_e}=0

Then we can get next relation - Beta equilibrium

\therefore \mu_n = \mu_p + \mu_e

And we can check that this momenta also maximize the pressure

\displaystyle \frac{\partial P}{\partial k_F} = 0, ~~\frac{\partial^2 P}{\partial k_F^2} < 0

Neutron Threshold

Let assume \( n_p = 0 = n_e \), then we can get \( k_n \) :

\mu _n = \mu_p + \mu_e \,\Rightarrow\,k_n^2 = (m_p + m_e)^2 - m_n^2 <0

Contradiction!

Let assume \( n_n=0 \), then we can get \( k_p \) :

\( \therefore \) At beta equilibrium, at least a small fraction of protons always exists.

\mu _n = \mu_p + \mu_e \,\Rightarrow\,k_p^2 = \left(\frac{m_n^2 - m_p^2 + m_e^2}{2m_n}\right)^2 - m_e^2 \simeq 1.4 \text{ MeV}^2

Thus, for \(n_B \leq 7.4919\times 10^{-9}\text{ fm}^{-3} \), a charge neutral Fermi gas in beta equilibrium is a equal mixture of \( p, e \), no \( n \)

n_B = n_p = \frac{k_p^3}{3\pi^2} \simeq 0.05757\text{ MeV}^3 \simeq 7.4919 \times 10^{-9} \text{ fm}^{-3}

"Neutron threshold"

"Neutron" Star

Now, see above the neutron threshold in ultra-relativistic regime.

\sqrt{k_n^2 + m_n^2} = \sqrt{k_p^2 + m_p^2} + \sqrt{k_e^2 + m_e^2} ~ \Rightarrow ~ k_n = 2k_p ~ \Rightarrow n_n = 8n_p

Thus, ignore other baryons, then

n_n = \dfrac{8}{9} n_B

Therefore most of baryons are neutrons.

\( \Rightarrow \) "Neutron" star

High Density & Low Density Limit

For High density, \( \dfrac{m}{k} \ll 1\)

\begin{aligned} &\varepsilon \rightarrow \dfrac{1}{4\pi^2} \left[k^4 - \frac{1}{2}m^4 \ln \frac{2k}{m}\right] \simeq \frac{1}{4\pi^2}k^4 \\ &P\rightarrow \frac{1}{12\pi^2} \left[ k^4 + \frac{3}{2}m^4\ln \frac{2k}{m}\right] \simeq \frac{1}{12\pi^2}k^4 \end{aligned}
\varepsilon = 3P \simeq \dfrac{1}{4\pi^2}(3\pi^2 n)^{4/3}

For Low density, \( \dfrac{k}{m} \ll 1\)

\begin{aligned} &\varepsilon \rightarrow \dfrac{m^4}{3\pi^2} \left(\dfrac{k^3}{m^3} \right) + \dfrac{m^4}{10\pi^2}\left(\dfrac{k^5}{m^5} \right) \\ &P\rightarrow \dfrac{m^4}{15\pi^2}\left(\dfrac{k^5}{m^5}\right) \end{aligned}
\varepsilon = m n + \dfrac{3}{2}P,~~P = \dfrac{(3\pi^2n)^{5/3}}{15\pi^2 m}

Brief View of FERMI GAS EOS

  1. \( \varepsilon \) < Neutron threshold ~ \(10^7 \text{ g/cm}^3\)
    : No neutron \(\rightarrow\) small \(k_p\) \(\rightarrow\) \(\varepsilon \simeq mn\)
  2. At Neutron Threshold
    : Increase \(n_n\) but \(n_n\simeq 0\)
    \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial \varepsilon}{\partial p}\simeq \dfrac{1}{n_n} \rightarrow \infty\) \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial P}{\partial \varepsilon} \simeq 0\)
  3. \(\varepsilon \gg\) Neutron threshold
    : High density \(\rightarrow \varepsilon \simeq 3P\)
